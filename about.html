My full name is Alexander Manuel Vallejo, and I was born on December 3rd, 1992. I have lived my entire life around the Pittsburgh area. I attended Our Lady of the Sacred Heart High School where I was the director of the school news studio, a member of NHS, and a varsity volleyball player. Also while in high school I was an active member in Boy Scout Troop 335 and achieved the rank of Eagle Scout at age fifteen. I began attending the Swanson School of Engineering at the University of Pittsburgh in the fall of 2011.

In college I major in computer engineering and maintain a GPA in the top 5% of my department while participating in several extracurricular activities. I have served as: Secretary of my fraternity (Delta Chi), Freshmen Engineering Peer adviser, Technical Director of the Green Hope Orphanage Inc, and president of the Pitt chapter of IEEE.

In the summer of 2013 I worked as a software engineer intern for KIT Solutions where I developed an internal timesheet application. In December of 2013 I achieved a long time life goal of mine and was offered a software engienering internship at Microsoft. In the summer of 2014 I worked on the OneDrive team and I built notifications for users of OneDrive who have expiring/expired bonus storage. My code is run about 3.5 million times a day and it maintains a quality of service of 99.998%. I will be moving back to Seattle in September of 2015 where I will return to Microsoft as a full-time engineer on the OneDrive team. I'm incredibly excited to move to Seattle! It has been a dream of mine to work for Microsoft (or another top tech company) for six years. Lots of hard work, determination and especially persistence got me to where I am today.
